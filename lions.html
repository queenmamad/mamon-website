Katanga Lion

The Kantaga lion (Panthera leo bleyenberghi), commonly known as the Southwest, is one of the several species of lions and has unique traits. It is possible for this huge lion subspecies to weigh up to 280 kilos. When it comes to appearance, the Kantaga lion stands out for having a distinctive, imposing mane that is sand in color. Light brown and coffee-colored variations of the mane's outermost region are possible.

Transvaal Lion

South Africa is home to the Panthera leo krugeri, also known as the Transvaal lion. This African lion, a species from the continent's southern region, is regarded as the Kantaga lion's sibling. Male Transvaal lions can grow as long as 2 meters and 50 centimeters. Even though their fur is typically sand-colored, this breed occasionally produces white lions. A recessive gene causes white fur to develop, thus white lion is a mutation of the krugeri. White lions are vulnerable in the wild despite their obvious beauty, especially since their pale coloring makes them impossible to conceal on the savanna.

Asiatic Lion

Although it is now present in zoos and reserves around the globe, the Asiatic lion (Panthera leo persica) is indigenous to India. This species of lion is smaller than other lion species, has a lighter coat, and the males have a reddish mane. Due to habitat loss, hunting, and competition with other animals living in their area, the Asiatic lion is one of the lion species that are currently in risk of going extinct.

Photo Credits: Chanakya Mandal Online

Congo Lion

A lion subspecies found throughout the plains of Africa, particuarly in Uganda and the Republic the Congo, is the Congo lion (Panthera leo azandica), commonly known as the central African lion. The dimensions of the Congo lion range from 2 meters and 50 centimeters. Additionally, this lion subspecies has a weight range of 150-190 kilograms. Male Congo lions are distinguished from other lion species by their their distinctive dark mane, which is less bushy. Its fur might be sand or dark brown in color.
